The Problem

Why Your Store Earns Less Than It Should

Most e-commerce brands pour budget into acquiring customers and then lose them silently after the first purchase. The revenue is already in your database — it's just not being activated.

Abandoned Carts Leaving Money on the Table

The average e-commerce store loses 70–75% of would-be transactions to cart abandonment. Most of those shoppers weren't saying no — they got distracted, had a question, or hit a friction point. Without a recovery sequence, that revenue is gone permanently.

One-Time Buyers Who Never Come Back

Industry data shows that 60–70% of e-commerce customers never make a second purchase. Yet retaining an existing customer costs 5–7x less than acquiring a new one. Without a post-purchase system to drive the second buy, you're running an acquisition treadmill with a leaky bucket.

No Systematic Post-Purchase Experience

After the confirmation email, most customers hear nothing until the next promotional blast. That silence is a missed window to upsell, cross-sell, gather a review, build loyalty, and prime a repeat purchase — all moments that compound directly into LTV.

Manual Customer Support Eating Team Time

Order status, shipping questions, return policies, and basic product FAQs consume 40–60% of e-commerce support tickets. When those are handled manually, your team can't focus on complex issues, VIP customers, or anything that actually moves revenue.

What We Build

Six AI Revenue Systems That Run Around the Clock

Each system integrates natively with your e-commerce platform and begins generating revenue from day one — without requiring your team to manage sequences manually.

Abandoned Cart Recovery Sequences

A three-touch recovery sequence deploys automatically when a shopper adds to cart and exits without purchasing. The first message goes out within 1 hour with a direct return link. The second, at 24 hours, addresses common objections. The third, at 72 hours, may include a time-limited incentive. Recovery rate averages 34% of abandoned carts — revenue that otherwise disappears.

Post-Purchase Upsell & Cross-Sell Automation

Within 48 hours of purchase delivery, customers receive a personalized recommendation sequence based on what they bought. Complementary products, consumable replenishment reminders, and bundle offers are served at the highest-intent moment in the customer lifecycle — immediately after a successful first experience with your brand.

Lapsed Customer Win-Back Campaigns

Customers who haven't purchased in 90, 180, or 365 days — depending on your average purchase frequency — receive a re-engagement sequence that acknowledges the gap, highlights what's new, and includes an exclusive returning-customer offer. Win-back campaigns routinely reactivate 10–18% of dormant buyers at a fraction of new acquisition cost.

VIP & Repeat Buyer Programs

Customers who cross a purchase frequency or spend threshold automatically enter a VIP track — early access to launches, exclusive offers, priority support, and personalized check-ins. VIP programs increase purchase frequency by 30–45% among enrolled customers and dramatically improve retention among your highest-value segment.

Review & UGC Collection

Timed to product delivery plus 5 days — when the customer experience is freshest — CNAX AI sends a review request with a direct product link and optional UGC prompt. For photo-driven categories, the system includes an easy mobile upload flow for customer photos. Stores see a 3–4x increase in review volume and meaningful organic UGC within the first 60 days.

AI Customer Support Automation

Order status lookups, shipping timeline questions, return policy explanations, and your top 20 FAQ responses are handled automatically — instantly, at any hour. Complex issues are escalated to your team with full context pre-loaded. Support ticket volume drops 40–60%, and your team spends their time on issues that actually require human judgment.
