The Real Problem

Your Trucks Are Available.
Your Pipeline Isn't.

Most HVAC companies lose business not from poor technician quality — but from systems that respond too slowly, don't follow up, and can't generate steady demand between seasons.

Losing Jobs to Faster Competitors

When a homeowner's AC dies in July, they call 3–5 companies. The first one that responds and confirms availability wins the job. If your team doesn't answer immediately — or can't respond within a few minutes — that $4,000–$12,000 system replacement goes to whoever picked up first. Speed is now your primary competitive advantage, not reputation.

Maintenance Agreements Not Renewing

Maintenance agreements are the lifeblood of a stable HVAC business — predictable revenue, priority customers, and the best source of replacement leads. But most companies let agreements lapse quietly because nobody follows up at the right time. Sending a renewal notice once by mail isn't a system; it's hoping customers remember you exist.

Seasonal Revenue Swings With No Pipeline

Peak season is chaotic, slow season is terrifying — and most HVAC companies swing between the two without a system to smooth the curve. Spring AC tune-up season and fall heating season are the two most predictable revenue windows of the year, yet most companies leave tens of thousands on the table by not reaching their existing customer base at the right moment.

Technician Schedules With Gaps

Idle technicians are the most expensive line item on your P&L. When cancellations happen or slow season hits, those unfilled time slots represent direct labor cost with zero revenue offset. Without a proactive system filling gaps with maintenance calls and tune-ups, you're paying your team to wait — and your competitors are calling your customers instead.

What We Automate

Six AI Systems That
Keep Your Trucks Running

Each system is configured around your service area, your pricing, and your seasonality — not a generic template built for someone else's market.

Instant Lead Response (90 Seconds)

Every inbound lead — web form, Google Local Services, Facebook ad, or missed call — gets an AI-powered response within 90 seconds, 24 hours a day. The response confirms receipt, asks qualifying questions about the system type and issue, and offers immediate scheduling. Your dispatcher is notified the moment a lead is hot and ready to book.

Maintenance Agreement Renewal Automation

We track every maintenance agreement in your system and trigger a multi-touch renewal sequence 45, 30, and 14 days before expiration — via SMS and email. Customers renew online in under two minutes. Lapsed agreements trigger a win-back sequence. Companies using this system see 54% higher renewal rates compared to mail-only outreach.

Seasonal Tune-Up Campaigns

Every March we run your spring AC tune-up campaign. Every September we run your fall heating inspection campaign. Both go out to your full customer database — past service clients, maintenance agreement holders, and dormant customers — with personalized messaging, seasonal urgency, and direct online booking. Most companies book 80–140 additional tune-ups per campaign cycle.

Emergency Service Lead Prioritization

When a lead indicates an emergency — no heat in winter, AC failure in a heat wave, gas smell, water damage — our system automatically flags it, bypasses the standard queue, and sends an immediate alert to your on-call dispatcher or technician. Emergency jobs are your highest-value calls. They should never wait in a general inbox.

Technician Review Collection

Two hours after a job is marked complete in your field service software, the customer gets a personalized SMS thanking them by technician name and requesting a Google review. The link goes directly to your Google Business Profile. Satisfied customers review in under 60 seconds. Customers who indicate a problem are routed to your service manager before they post publicly.

Dormant Customer Re-Engagement

Customers who haven't called in 2+ years are identified and entered into a re-engagement sequence. We reach out with value-first messaging — a reminder about manufacturer warranty service intervals, a system age check-in, or a seasonal efficiency tip — and invite them back with a service discount. This consistently recovers 12–20% of dormant customers within 45 days.
